Figures

Figure 1: Length of stories in Thiele’s Danmarks folkesagn, measured in tokens, and binned into groups of 50 tokens. Across the top, a simple box plot showing the main range and concentration of story lengths.

Figure 2a: A bipartite layout for the ego-network representation of topic 1, “the minister, the king, came, last, stood” expanded to include the first topic neighbors of all the associated legends. Note that the topic space of 44 topics is fully covered by this selection of legends

Figure 2b: A selection of all the legends classified by Thiele as “holy springs”, and their associated topics. Note that not all the legends in Thiele’s class are captured by the very fine-grained topic, “spring, mile, field, told, carries, said, water”.

Figure 3: The intertopic distance map visualizer, showing the overall topic space, where distance between topics is measured using a cosine similarity metric, and size captures the number of associated legend chunks. Topics that share a great deal of semantic similarity are presented close to one another in this 2-D representation of the clustered 5-D space.

Figure 4: A dendrogram visualization of the topic space, offering an alternative hierarchical representation of the collection that can supplement Thiele’s initial classification. Here, one of the nodes higher in the hierarchy is highlighted, labeled with the highest ranked terms from the leaf nodes, capturing the relationship between ministers and ghosts, including those of dead children.

Figure 5a: The unclustered bipartite graph, using a spring embedded layout algorithm. The larger topic nodes (sized by degree) are at the center of most stars (light green), while the stories are represented by smaller nodes—the color of the node represents its Thiele class-subclass label, while the outer ring is the Thiele division (four main divisions).

Figure 5b: A clustered version of the same graph, using a fast-greedy modularity-based clustering algorithm (Girvan & Newman 2002).

Figure 6: A close-up of one of the legend-topic clusters from the bipartite graph. Topics are labeled with their top ten words, and stories are labeled with their title. While some stories have a single association to a topic, such as “En nedmanet genganger i Ebeltoft” [A conjured revenant in Ebeltoft], others, such as “Røverne i Vissenbjerg” [The Robbers in Vissenbjerg], play a bridging function between topics.

Figure 7a: A maximal spanning tree, created using Kruskal’s algorithm and making use of edge weights. The tree covers all the legends and connects them with their highest weighted edge. A story, such as “Skatten i Sortenshave” is identified as having very high degree in this network representation, identifying it as a target for additional investigation.

Figure 7b: Six possible high weight paths from the story “Jomfruspringet” [Maiden leap] to the story “Rørvig Kirke” [Rørvig church].
